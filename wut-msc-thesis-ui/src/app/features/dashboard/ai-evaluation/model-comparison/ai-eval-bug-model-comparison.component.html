What you should NOT use

🚫 Pie chart (bad choice here)

Pie charts are for proportions of a whole. Our data mixes time, variability, estimation accuracy, and stability, so there is no 100% whole to split.

👉 A pie chart would be misleading and any examiner would question it.

Best charts for THIS data
  • 🥇 Grouped bar chart

    Gemini vs DeepSeek on avg analysis time, avg estimated hours, stability score, and confidence level.

  • 🥈 Range (min–max) bar

    Highlights uncertainty envelopes: min→max analysis time and min→max estimated hours.

  • 🥉 Error bar chart

    Avg analysis time ± confidence interval. Screams “statistically grounded evaluation”.

  • 4️⃣ Radar (optional)

    Nice for slides, but secondary to the evidence-based charts above.

Required combo: grouped bar + error bar, with range-bar as supporting evidence.
